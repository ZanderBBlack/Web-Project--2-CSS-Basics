- - - Treaty Number 3 - - -

Who was involved with this Treaty?

The government of Canada was a main center point commited to a treaty with Indigenous communities included the Saulteaux (Anishinaabe & Ojibwe People) and others. Some communties that share the benefits and obligation of this treaty included Falcon Lake, Middlebro, Point du Bois, as well as much of Whiteshell.
( The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3 & Southerns Chief Orgnaization's Inc- Treaties )

When and where did this Treaty take place?

Treaty 3 was negotiated in late July, 1871 at Fort Frances and the Shebandowan Lakes. It took another 2 years until the treaty came to take place on October 3rd, 1873.
( The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3 )

What was the context of the Treaty? Why did it take place?

The federal government wanted access to the Objiwe lands in present day northwestern Ontario and eastern Manitoba. In 1869 the government began making a road and waterway system with the primary objective to provide access to the Canadian Interior. The Ojibwe would "cede, release, surrender and yield" all rights to their land to the Governement of Canada in echange for various good and Indigenous rights to hunting and fishing. This land included an area measuring 14,245,000 hectares (ha) which the governemnt had intentions of turning into farming land in the future.
( Grand Council Treaty 3 & The Canadian Encyclopedia- Treaty 3 )

How did this Treaty come to be?

The federal government appointed Robert Pither, a former Hudson's Bay employee, to be sent to the Objiwe as an Indian agent (a government representitve on Indigenous reserves) in 1870. He was then sent to Fort Frances of northwestern Ontario, where he was to “establish and keep upfriendly relations” with the Indigenous peoples. The Ojibwe were ready to negotiate a treaty, they made it clear to Wemyss Simpson (an appointed Indian commissioner with the authority to make treaties) that they had no intention of permitting “farmers to settle” on their land. Indicating that they did not intend to cede their rights to the land and wanted compensation exchange for allowing the development of specific government projects on their territory. They later came to a negotiation however many promises werent kept.
( The Canadian Encyclopedia- Treaty 3 & Grand Council Treaty 3 & Government of Canada- Treaty 3 )

Terms of the Treaty:

Under the terms of the treaty, the Government of Canada promised to set aside reserves for the Ojibwe and to provide them with various financial gains, including a one-time cash payment of $12 per family of five and a yearly payment of $5 per person. These key Crown obligations which were of higher allowance apposed to previous treaties at that time.
( The Canadian Encyclopedia- Treaty 3 )

What came after?

Treaty 3 became the standard by which the rest of the Numbered Treaties were modeled government also raised the annual payments given to the Indigenous peoples of Treaties 1 and 2 so as to better reflect those of Treaty 3. Later Treaty 3 gained its own police force "the Treaty Three Police Service (TTPS)". Funded by the federal and provincial government.
( The Canadian Encyclopedia- Treaty 3 )
