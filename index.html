- - - Treaty Number 3 - - -

Who was involved with this Treaty?

The government of Canada was a main center point committed to a treaty with Indigenous communities including the Saulteaux (Anishinaabe & Ojibwe People) and others. Some communities that share the benefits and obligation of this treaty included Falcon Lake, Middlebro, Point du Bois, as well as much of Whiteshell. (The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3 & Southern Chiefs Organization's Inc- Treaties)

When and where did this Treaty take place?

Treaty 3 was negotiated in late July, 1871 at Fort Frances and the Shebandowan Lakes. It took another 2 years until the treaty was signed on October 3rd, 1873. (The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3)

What was the context of the Treaty? Why did it take place?

The federal government wanted access to the Ojibwe lands in present-day northwestern Ontario and eastern Manitoba. The government began making a road and waterway system in 1869 to provide access to the Canadian Interior. The Ojibwe would "cede, release, surrender, and yield" all rights to their land in exchange for goods and Indigenous rights to hunting and fishing. This land measured 14,245,000 hectares which the government intended to turn into farming land. (Grand Council Treaty 3 & The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3)

How did this Treaty come to be?

The federal government appointed Robert Pither, a former Hudson's Bay employee, as an Indian agent in 1870 to maintain relations with the Ojibwe. After some negotiation, the treaty was signed, though many promises were later unfulfilled. (The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3 & Grand Council Treaty 3 & Government of Canada-Treaty 3)

Terms of the Treaty:

Under the terms of the treaty, the government promised to provide reserves and financial benefits to the Ojibwe. These included a one-time payment of $12 per family of five and a yearly payment of $5 per person. These terms were more generous than those offered in previous treaties. (The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3)

What came after?

Treaty 3 became the model for other Numbered Treaties, influencing the terms of Treaties 1 and 2. It also led to the creation of the Treaty Three Police Service (TTPS) funded by the federal and provincial government. (The Canadian Encyclopedia-Treaty 3)
